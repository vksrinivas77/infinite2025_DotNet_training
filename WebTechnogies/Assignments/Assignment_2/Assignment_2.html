Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (1931–2015) was a visionary Indian scientist, aerospace engineer, and the 11th President of India (2002–2007). Rising from humble beginnings in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he became known as the "Missile Man of India" for his leadership in the development of India's missile and nuclear weapons programs.

Notable Achievements

  • Chief architect of India's Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP), leading to Agni and Prithvi missiles.
  • Pivotal role in Pokhran-II nuclear weapons tests in 1998, making India a recognized nuclear power.
  • Key figure in launching India’s first satellite vehicle (SLV-III) and placing the Rohini satellite in orbit (1980).
  • Served as the 11th President of India, fondly known as the “People’s President”.
  • Authored popular books including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds".
  • Awarded Bharat Ratna (1997), Padma Vibhushan (1990), and Padma Bhushan (1981).
